What is Kaitiakitanga?

He ariā Māori a Kaitiakitanga e whakatinana ana i nga tikanga o te kaitiaki me te tuari pūmau o te taiao. I pūtaketia i runga i te manaaki hōhonu mo te āhua, e whakahua ana te kaitiakitanga i te kawenga a te tangata ki te tiaki i te whenua, i te wai, me ngā mea ora katoa, me te whakarite i to rātau tiakitanga mo nga whakatupuranga ā muri ake. Ko tēnei huarahi katoa e whakakotahi ana i ngā uara ahurea, wairua, rauropi hoki, e tautohe ana ki tētahi pātahitanga hāngai i waenganui i ngā tāngata me te ao tūturu. Mā te whakamātau Kaitiakitanga, ko ngā hapori e tautāwhi ana i ngā tikanga o te pūmautanga me te whakahoki, e whāngai ana i tētahi noho whārite ki te taiao
Kaitiakitanga is a Māori concept that embodies the principles of guardianship and sustainable stewardship of the environment. Rooted in deep respect for nature, kaitiakitanga emphasizes the responsibility of humans to care for the land, water, and all living things, ensuring their preservation for future generations. This holistic approach integrates cultural, spiritual, and ecological values, advocating for a harmonious relationship between people and the natural world. By practicing kaitiakitanga, communities uphold traditions of sustainability and reverence, fostering a balanced coexistence with the environment.
